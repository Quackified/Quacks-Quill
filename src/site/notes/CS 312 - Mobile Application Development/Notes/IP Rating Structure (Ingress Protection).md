To achieve water resistance, a device typically needs an IP Rating where the second digit indicates liquid protection which is required to be sufficiently high.

An IP (Ingress Protection) rating consists of two digits:
* The first digit indicates protection against solid objects (like dust).
* The second digit indicates protection against liquids (like water).

Common IP Ratings for Water Resistance:
1. IPx4 - Splash Resistant
   Devices with an IPx4 rating are protected against splasher of water from any direction. This means the device can handle light rain or splashes but is not fully water-resistant.
2. IPx5 & IPx6 - Water Jet Resistant
   IPx5 - Protection against water jets from any direction. This means the device can handle being sprayed w/ water, but immersion is not safe.
   IPx6 - Protection against more powerful fountains, strong/powerful water jets. Devices with this rating can withstand more intense water exposure but are still not safe for immersion (deeply engage absorbed).
3. IPx7 - Water Resistant (Temporary Immersion)
   Devices with an IPx7 rating can be submerged in water up to 1 meter (3 feet) for up to 30 minutes. This rating is common for smartphones that can handle brief accidental drops into water.
4. IPx8 - Water Resistant (Longer or Deeper Immersion)
   Devices with an IPx8 rating can be submerged in water deeper than 1 meter for a longer period. The exact depth and time are usually specified by the manufacturer. This is often seen in devices designed for more rigorous underwater use, like certain rugged smartphones or cameras.
5. IP68 - Dustproof and Water Resistant
   One of the most common ratings for high-end smartphones. The "6" indicates full-dust protection, and the "8" indicates water resistance for immersion beyond 1 meter, typically 1.5 meters for 30 minutes. This rating means the device is both dustproof and can handle accidental submersion in water.

##### How to check IP Rating on your Phone?

1. Manufacturer's website or User Manual
2. Phone Settings (rare, unlikely) - About Phone
3. Online Reviews and Retailers
4. Contact Customer Support
5. Third-Party Databases (GSMArena or PhoneArena)
