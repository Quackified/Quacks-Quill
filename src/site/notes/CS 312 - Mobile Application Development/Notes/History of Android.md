#### October 2003 – 2005

Android Inc. was founded in **Palo Alto, California** which the founders were **Andy Rubin, Rich Miner, Nick Sears, Chris White**. In which Rubin played a significant role in developing Android operating system.

Originally aimed at digital camera, but the company shifted (pivot) focus to **mobile phones** as they saw the smartphone market's potential.

#### August 2005

- Google buys Android Inc.
- Many from the original team joined Google
  
> [!info] Correction
> Actually, the Android Team officially moved to Google's campus in **Mountain View, California** on **July 11, 2005**. That day is _considered_ the official date for the acquisition of Android by Google. However, the news about Google purchasing Android didn’t become public until a few weeks later, in August 2005.
###### Why did Google bought Android Inc?

- There were still no iPhones back then, but Google saw opportunity of the usability of smartphones especially through interconnectivity.
- Wanted to expand not only on Desktops, but also on Mobile Phones as well. They want to be relevant in the era of mobile phones.
- Before, the Mobile OS Market was dominated by Symbian OS (Nokia) and Windows Mobile (Microsoft), if Google doesn't do anything, there's a big possibility that Google services in mobile will be taken over by Microsoft and other search apps.

##### What technique did they use?

- The Android Team planned to make the OS be open-source and free to all phone manufacturers to bring many users as much as possible and adapt on their platform. 
- Google Search and Ads will be the "default", since Google is the number one in terms of online advertisement. The philosophy being since they have their own OS, they will use that to integrate their own applications like Google Ads, Gmail, Youtube, Google Search in their mobile environment. 

	More users that uses their service = More Revenue

##### Why did Android Inc. allowed Google to buy the OS?

- Android Inc. before was only a small Startup back then, they lack funds, resources, and even connections to develop the OS that they envisioned.
- With Google, they can use Engineering teams, Data centers global network that Startups cannot provide. Allows a faster development & higher product quality.

#### November 2007 – 2008

**Open Handset Alliance (OHA)** - First release of Android on **November 05, 2007** — Google announces Android publicly, alongside the OHA (Initially 34 Companies).

##### Purpose of OHA

- To speed up the deployment of Android on newer phones, distributing to many users on the market which allowed market dominance.
- Easier to deploy Android on new phones. If there were no OHA, there's a possibility that Android would just be strictly for Google-only, same as Apple strictly being iPhone (IOS) - because of OHA it became a Global ecosystem that is used by many brands.

##### Members of OHA
Initially there were only 34 Companies that partake the alliance then by 2026, it gradually grew to 84 Companies.

1. **Device Manufacturers** - They design and assemble phones; prominent companies like HTC, Samsung, LG, Motorola, Sony Ericsson, etc.
2. **Chipset Makers** - Qualcomm (Snapdragon), Texas Instruments (Now pivoted to Automotive Electronics, Medical Devices, Factory Automation), Broadcom (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth Chipsets)
3. **Mobile Operators** - T-Mobile, Sprint, China Mobile
4. **Software Companies** - Google, eBay
5. Others - **Telecom companies**, **Hardware suppliers**

##### Android Logo (2007 – 2008 named Bugdroid)

Google decided that they needed a *mascot/logo* that was easy to remember.

**2007** - During November 5, 2007, **Irina Blok** was the graphic designer who designed **Bugdroid**. She based it of the restroom signage, they wanted it simple. Green Robot with antennas and 2 dots for the eyes.

**2019** - It was re-designed during the release of Android 10. Logo became head-only instead of full-body, it was more minimalistic and flexible.

#### September 2008

The release of the first Android Phone: HTC Dream/T-Mobile G1 (Android 1.0), features included Web Browsing, Gmail Sync, Google Maps, Youtube, Android

**2014** - Android Wear, Android Auto, Android Market (Now Google Play Store), Android TV

#### Trivia

- **Samsung** was the manufacturer of the iPhone screens.
- Samsung Display is the biggest supplier of OLED (Organic Light-Emitting Diode) on iPhones.
- **OLED Behavior** - If Black is needed = Pixels are off, if White/Other Colors = combinations of Pixels are ON.

##### OLED vs LCD

- OLED screens functions on pixels – LCDs require Backlight on the back.
- OLED screens has purer black – LCDs are more grayer than black (also may Backlight Bleed)

**AMOLED** (Active Matrix OLED) - More advanced than regular OLED
**Super AMOLED** - Samsung AMOLED – integrated touch-sensitive layer instead of requiring a separate touch layer.
